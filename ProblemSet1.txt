\section*{Problem 1}

In the following question, the domain of {\bf discourse} is a set of male patients in a clinical study. Define the following predicates:\\
\begin{itemize}
  \item $P(x):\; x$ was given the placebo\\\\
  \item $D(x):\; x$ was given the medication\\\\
  \item $M(x): \; x$ had migraines\\\\
\end{itemize}
Translate each of the following statements into a logical expression. Then negate the expression by adding a negation operation to the beginning of the expression. Apply De Morgan's law until each negation operation applies directly to a predicate and then translate the logical expression back into English.\\\\

{\it
Sample question: Some patient was given the placebo and the medication.\\
\begin{itemize}
  \item $\exists x\; (P(x)\; \land \; D(x))$\\
  \item Negation: $\neg \exists x\; (P(x)\; \land \; D(x))$\\
  \item Applying De Morgan's law: $\forall x\; (\neg P(x)\; \lor \; \neg D(x))$\\
  \item English: Every patient was either not given the placebo or not given the medication (or both).\\
\end{itemize}
}
\newpage
%--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]

\item Every patient was given the medication or the placebo or both.\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\forall x ((D(x)\lor P(x))\lor (D(x)\land P(x)))$  \\
    \item Negation: $\neg\forall x ((D(x)\lor P(x)) \lor (D(x)\land (P(x)))$ \\
       \item Applying De Morgan's law: \\
    \begin{align*}
        &\neg\forall x (D(x) \lor P(x)) \lor (D(x) \land P(x)) \\
        &\exists x \neg((D(x) \lor P(x)) \lor (D(x) \land P(x))) \\
        &\exists x \neg(D(x) \lor P(x)) \land \neg(D(x) \land P(x)) \\
        &\exists x (\neg D(x) \land \neg P(x)) \lor (\neg D(x) \lor \neg P(x)) \\
    \end{align*}
    \item English: There exists a patient that either did not receive both the medication or placebo, or did not receive either the medication or placebo.
  \end{itemize}
\\\\\

\item Every patient who took the placebo had migraines. (Hint: you will need to apply the conditional identity, $p \to q \equiv \neg p \lor q$.)\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\forall x ((P(x))\rightarrow (M(x)))$ \\
    \item Negation: \\
    \begin{align*}
        \neg \forall x ((P(x))\rightarrow (M(x))) &\equiv
             \neg \forall x (\neg P(x) \lor M(x)) \\
        &\equiv \exists x \neg(\neg(P(x))\lor(M(x))\\
    \end{align*}  
    \item Applying De Morgan's law: \\
    \begin{align*}
        \exists x \neg(\neg P(x)\lor M(x))
        \equiv& \exists x (\neg\neg P(x)\land\neg M(x)) \\
        \equiv& \exists x (P(x) \land \neg M(x))
    \end{align*}\\
    \item English: There exists a patient who took the placebo and did not have a migraine. \\
  \end{itemize}
\\\\
\item There is a patient who had migraines and was given the placebo.\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
  \begin{itemize}
    \item $\exists x (M(x)\land P(x))$ \\
    \item Negation: \\
    \begin{align*}
        \neg \exists(M(x) \land P(x))\\
        \end{align*}  
    \item Applying De Morgan's law: \\
    \begin{align*}
        \neg \exists(M(x) \land P(x))&\equiv \forall x \neg (M(x) \land P(x))\\
        &\equiv \forall x (\neg M(x) \lor \neg P(x))\\
    \end{align*}\\
    \item English: All patients had neither a migraine nor a placebo nor both. \\
  \end{itemize}
\\\\
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Problem 2}

Use De Morgan's law for quantified statements and the laws of propositional logic to show the following equivalences:\\
\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]
\item $\neg \forall x \, \left(P(x) \land \neg Q(x) \right)\; \equiv \; \exists x \, \left(\neg P(x) \lor  Q(x) \right)$\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
\begin{align*}
    \neg \forall x (P(x) \land \neg Q(x)) &\equiv \exists x (\neg P(x) \lor Q(x))\\
    \exists x (\neg P(x) \lor \neg \neg Q(x)) &\equiv\\
    \exists x (\neg P(x) \lor Q(x)) &\equiv\\
\end{align*}
\\\\
\item $\neg \forall x \, \left(\neg P(x) \to Q(x) \right)\; \equiv \; \exists x \, \left(\neg P(x) \land  \neg Q(x) \right)$\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
\begin{align*}
    \neg\forall x (\neg P(x)\rightarrow Q(x))&\equiv \exists x (\neg P(x) \land \neg Q(x))\\
    \exists x \neg (\neg P(x) \rightarrow Q(x))&\equiv\\
    \exists x \neg(\neg\neg P(x) \lor Q(x))&\equiv\\
    \exists x \neg(P(x) \lor Q(x))&\equiv\\
    \exists x (\neg P(x) \land \neg Q(x))&\equiv
\end{align*}
\\\\
\item $\neg \exists x \, \big(\neg P(x) \lor \left(Q(x) \land \neg R(x) \right)\big)\; \equiv \; \forall x \,\big( P(x) \land \left( \neg Q(x) \lor R(x) \right)\big)$\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
\begin{align*}
    \neg \exists x (\neg P(x) \lor (Q(x) \land \neg R(x)))&\equiv \forall x (P(x)\land (\neg Q(x)\lor R(x)))\\
    \forall x \neg (\neg P(x) \lor (Q(x) \land\neg R(x)))&\equiv\\
    \forall x (\neg\neg P(x) \land \neg (Q(x) \land \neg R(x)))&\equiv\\
    \forall x (P(x) \land \neg (Q(x)\land \neg R(x)))&\equiv\\
    \forall x (P(x) \land (\neg Q(x) \lor \neg\neg R(x)))&\equiv\\
    \forall x (P(x) \land (\neg Q(x) \lor R(x)))&\equiv\\
\end{align*}
\\\\
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Problem 3}

The domain of {\bf discourse} for this problem is a group of three people who are working on a project. To make notation easier, the people are numbered $1, \;2, \;3$. The predicate $M(x,\; y)$ indicates whether x has sent an email to $y$, so $M(2, \;3)$ is read ``Person $2$ has sent an email to person $3$.'' The table below shows the value of the predicate $M(x,\;y)$ for each $(x,\;y)$ pair. The truth value in row $x$ and column $y$ gives the truth value for $M(x,\;y)$.\\\\
\[
 \begin{array}{||c||c|c|c||}
\hline\hline
M & 1 & 2& 3\\
\hline\hline
1 &T & T & T\\
\hline
2 &T & F & T\\
\hline
3 &T & T & F\\
\hline\hline
    \end{array}
    \]\\\\
{\bf Determine if the quantified statement is true or false. Justify your answer.}\\

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]

\item $\forall x \, \forall y \left(x\not= y)\;\to \;  M(x,\;y)\right)$\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
\begin{itemize}
    \item This is true. For every pair x and y, if x and y are different people, then person x has sent an email to person y. All of the non diagonal items in the truth table are true.\\\\
\end{itemize}

\item $\forall x \, \exists y \;\; \neg M(x,\;y)$\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
\begin{itemize}
    \item This is false. The statement says for all people x, there exists a person y that person x did not send an email to. This is false. Person labeled 1 did send an email to all people and does not satisfy the statement to make it true.\\\\ 
\end{itemize}

\item $\exists x \, \forall y \;\; M(x,\;y)$\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.  
\begin{itemize}
    \item This statement is true. The statement says that there is a person x who for all people y were sent an email. Person 1 sent an email to all people, making this statement a true statement.\\\\
\end{itemize}
\end{enumerate}

\begin{enumerate}[label=(\alph*)]

\item The reciprocal of every positive number less than one is greater than one.\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.

    \item $\forall x (((x<1)\land (x>0)) \rightarrow (\frac{1}{x}>1))$\\\\
\end{itemize}
\item There is no smallest number.\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.

    \item $\neg \exists x \forall y (x\leq y)$\\\\
\end{itemize}
\item Every number other than 0 has a multiplicative inverse.\\\\
%Enter your answer below this comment line.
\begin{itemize}
    \item $\forall x \exists y ((x \neq 0) \rightarrow (xy=1))$
\end{itemize}\\\\
\end{enumerate}
